I am a Doctoral Candidate in Urban and Regional Sciences at Texas A&M University. My research broadly focuses on housing affordability; specifically, developing new models to measure owner- or renter-occupied housing affordability. I am also building a framework — the first of its kind — to model owner-occupied housing affordability and neighborhood opportunity concurrently.

I currently work alongside Research Economists at the Texas Real Estate Research Center to estimate housing needs for communities across Texas. I received my masters degree in Land Economics and Real Estate from Texas A&M University in 2017 and my undergraduate degrees in Geography and Urban Studies, with honors, from the University of Texas at Austin in 2016.
